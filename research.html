Research

Current work combines first-principles theory, atomistic modeling, and data-driven potential development to understand heat transport and structure–property relationships in materials.

Area 1

First-principles materials modeling

Using density functional theory and related approaches to study electronic, structural, and energetic properties of materials systems.

Area 2

Machine-learning interatomic potentials

Building efficient atomistic models for semiconductors and heterostructures to extend simulation scales beyond direct DFT.

Area 3

Phonons and thermal transport

Investigating phonon behavior, defect scattering, and thermal conductivity in complex materials and interfaces.

Research Cards

PbSe / PbTe and related systems

Potential development and validation for materials relevant to lattice dynamics and transport studies.

Defect-driven thermal resistance

Exploring how dislocations and microstructural features influence phonon scattering pathways.

Nanomaterials for sensing and adsorption

Prior work on low-dimensional materials for sensing, adsorption, and molecular interaction studies using DFT.
