Research

Current Work

My current doctoral research focuses on developing advanced machine-learning interatomic potentials to study phonon-dislocation interactions and their influence on phonon thermal transport in PbSe, PbTe, and PbSe/PbTe heterostructures. This work combines atomistic simulation, computational materials modeling, and thermal transport analysis.

Previous Research

My earlier work concentrated on first-principles investigations of low-dimensional nanomaterials, especially their use in drug delivery, gas sensing, and adsorption studies. These projects used Density Functional Theory to understand structural, electronic, and interaction properties.

Research Interests

  • Density Functional Theory
  • Machine-Learning Potentials
  • Phonon and Thermal Transport
